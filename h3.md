## c)

Now, I tried the `strings packd` command to see the packd binary files! This printed out binary files that looked different from the passtr binary files!


<img width="907" height="583" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/eb895db9-f78e-42f8-a0fd-7eee1ec67991" />

At first I thought it might be using a different encoding system, so I tried different variations of the strings command. Then I took a closer look at the binary-files and saw that
It started with UPX!! and ended with UPX!!

I searched for UPX on the internet and found UPX on github! From their page 

"Welcome to UPX !

UPX is a free, secure, portable, extendable, high-performance
executable packer for several executable formats."

Now I Installed UPX using `sudo dnf install upx`! Then I ran `upx -h` and found the `upx -d [filename]` command!

Now, after I ran `upx -d packd' in the /packd directory. This is what came out of `strings packd`!
